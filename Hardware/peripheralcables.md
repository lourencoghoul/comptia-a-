#  Peripheral Cables & Audio Connectors

##  What are they?

Peripheral cables are used to connect external devices (peripherals) to a computer.

---

##  Common Types

### USB (Universal Serial Bus)

* **USB Type-A (Universal Serial Bus Type-A)** → Standard connector for keyboards, mice, and flash drives
* **USB Type-B (Universal Serial Bus Type-B)** → Commonly used for printers and larger devices
* **USB Micro (Universal Serial Bus Micro)** → Used in older smartphones and small devices
* **USB Type-C (Universal Serial Bus Type-C)** → Modern reversible connector for data, video, and power

### Video

* **HDMI (High-Definition Multimedia Interface)** → Digital video and audio for monitors and TVs
* **DisplayPort (DisplayPort Interface)** → High-performance video connection
* **VGA (Video Graphics Array)** → Analog video (legacy)

### Network

* **Ethernet (RJ-45 – Registered Jack 45)** → Network and internet connection

### Audio (Digital & Analog)

* **3.5mm Jack (Analog Audio Jack)** → Standard analog audio connection
* **TOSLINK (Toshiba Link)** → Digital audio via optical fiber

### High-Speed Data

* **Thunderbolt (High-Speed I/O Interface)** → Data, video, and power (uses USB-C in newer versions)

---

##  Audio Connectors (3.5mm Color Code)

* *** Pink (Microphone In)** → Connects microphones (audio input)
* *** Green (Line Out / Audio Out)** → Connects speakers or headphones (audio output)
* *** Blue (Line In)** → Connects external audio sources (audio input)

---

##  Purpose

* Data transfer
* Audio and video transmission
* Device connectivity

---

##  Note

Different cables and connectors support different speeds, resolutions, and power delivery depending on their type and version. Modern devices may combine multiple functions into a single port.
